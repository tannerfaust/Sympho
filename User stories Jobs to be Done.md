# User stories / Jobs to be Done

### The "Capture" Job

- **User Story:** As a busy professional, I want to log a new learning topic via a single, distraction-free input field or global shortcut, so I can capture a knowledge gap instantly without breaking my flow state.
- **Product Focus:** Absolute minimal friction. No mandatory tagging, categorizing, or complex forms at the moment of entry. It goes straight to an inbox.

### The "Deconstruction" Job

- **User Story:** As a learner tackling a complex subject, I want the system to generate a visual, sequential tree of prerequisites, so I can identify exactly where my foundational knowledge needs to start.
- **Product Focus:** Clarity over complexity. The UI needs to present this breakdown cleanly, avoiding an overwhelming, cluttered mind-map look.

### The "Triage" Job

- **User Story:** As a busy professional with a large backlog, I want to quickly assign my saved topics to active projects or immediate goals, so I know exactly what to study this weekend to drive progress.
- **Product Focus:** Quick drag-and-drop or simple assignment mechanics to move items from the "Inbox" into an "Active Queue."

### The "Resource Convergence" Job

- **User Story:** As a learner entering a study session, I want to attach external links, PDFs, and videos directly to a specific topic node, so all my materials are unified in one dedicated workspace.
- **Product Focus:** Clean data architecture. The topic acts as the parent container holding all relevant files and links, eliminating the need to search through external folders or browser tabs.

### The "Context Switching" Job

- **User Story:** As a busy professional returning to the app, I want to see a clear "resume where you left off" indicator with my next immediate sub-topic, so I can instantly bypass setup time and dive straight into learning.
- **Product Focus:** A dashboard or home view that immediately surfaces the active, in-progress topic rather than a static list of all items.

### The "Knowledge Debt" Job

- **User Story:** As a professional encountering repeated issues, I want to flag a foundational topic as a critical blocker, so the system visibly prioritizes it at the top of my queue until I resolve it.
- **Product Focus:** A distinct visual state (like a high-priority tag or color code) that overrides standard sorting to force attention.

### The "Retention" Job

- **User Story:** As a learner who has completed a module, I want the system to surface a list of topics marked for review after a set period, so I can validate my recall and permanently cement the knowledge.
- **Product Focus:** A simple, automated "Review" queue that populates based on completion dates, keeping the logic straightforward for the MVP.
